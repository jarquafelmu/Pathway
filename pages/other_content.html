services missionaries

Missionaries play a central role in Pathway. Each Pathway site has one or more mission couples who encourage, motivate, and care for the students.

The missionaries introduce students to the program, guide them through the application and registration process, and mentor them throughout their Pathway experience. The missionaries’ wisdom and experience will help students navigate every aspect of the program, such as using the online courses, leading the gathering discussions, and accessing the many available student resources.

Above all, the missionaries cheer Pathway students on to success and encourage them to reach their potential by working hard, seeking the Lord’s guidance, and embracing new opportunities.

admission requirements

To be eligible to participate in Pathway, applicants must:

  • Be a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latterday Saints
  • Attend the weekly gathering during Pathway
  • Strive to live by the BYU-Idaho Honor Code
  • Be proficient in listening, speaking, reading, and writing English (proficiency exam may be required)
  • Be capable of entry-level college academic work with post-secondary disability accommodations, if qualified and required.
  • Be able to access a computer with high-speedinternet on a daily basis
  • Live within a reasonable commuting distance of a Pathway site.

Cost

Pathway’s low tuition rate brings a college education within financial reach of many who thought they could not afford college. Pathway students who pursue a degree through BYU-Idaho's Online Degree Program after Pathway continue to pay their local Pathway tuition rate.

  • Pathway tuition may increase annually at the same rate as other BYUIdaho students.
  • Pathway students who choose to continue their education on-campus after Pathway will pay regular on-campus tuition.
