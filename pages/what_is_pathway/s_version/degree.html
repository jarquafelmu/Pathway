Standard Version

For students who are proficient in English, the standard version combines BYU-Idaho online academic courses with either institute or BYU-Idaho religion courses. Students take one academic class and one religion class each of the three semesters.

Degree Options

Successfully completing Pathway opens doors to opportunity, which include participating in the BYU-Idaho Online Degree Program, attending BYU-Idaho as an on-campus student, or enrolling in a local college or university.

Online Degree Program

• At the end of Pathway, students may advance into BYU-Idaho's Online Degree Program. To be eligible for the Online Degree Program, students must earn a minimum "B" average (3.0 GPA) during Pathway and obtain an ecclesiastical endorsement from their bishop. Students will continue to take courses online at the same low Pathway tuition rate and work toward some or all of the following options:

  • Professional Certificates (credits)
  • Associate Degree (60 credits)
  • Bachelor’s Degree (120 credits)
Learn more about the BYU-Idaho Online Degree Program.

Campus

Students who complete Pathway and wish to attend BYU-Idaho on campus in Rexburg must apply at besmart.com and pay the regular campus tuition. If the student completed Pathway with at least a 3.0 GPA, the high school diploma and standardized test score (ACT/SAT) requirement will be waived as part of the application process.

Other Universities

Completion of Pathway will give students the confidence and skills needed to succeed in any college. Students are encouraged to attend local colleges and universities at the completion of Pathway if their educational and career goals are better met there. Admission requirements, tuition rates, and transfer credit requirements vary by institution.
