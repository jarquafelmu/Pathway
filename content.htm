How Pathway Works

Weekly Gathering

The program includes gathering once a week at a local institute facility or church meetinghouse with other Pathway students and a service missionary couple. In addition to providing social, emotional, and academic support, the gathering is a weekly assignment in the academic courses and fulfills the residency requirement to join the BYU-Idaho Online Degree Program at the conclusion of Pathway.

Gatherings are held every Thursday evening during the three BYU-Idaho academic semesters.


Online Academic Courses

During each of the three semesters, students take just one online academic class from BYU-Idaho as well as one religion class. The three academic classes are designed to give students the confidence and skills needed to succeed in college and include:

  • Pathway Life Skills (GS 120)
  • Basic Writing (ENG 106)
  • Personal Finance (Math 100G)

Religion Classes

Pathway students are divided into two group types: Age 18-30 and Age 31+.

Students in the Age 18-30 group complete their Book of Mormon courses through their local institute program at no charge, and the credits are then eligible for transfer to BYU-Idaho.

Students in the Age 31+ group complete their Book of Mormon courses online through BYU-Idaho.

Both groups complete the FDREL 333 course online through BYU-Idaho.
